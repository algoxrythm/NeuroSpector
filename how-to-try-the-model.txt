NEUROSPECTOR: THE BEGINNER'S GUIDE TO TESTING THE AI

Hello! If you have no programming experience and just want to see this AI
model work on your computer, you are in the right place.

Follow these steps exactly, and you'll have an industrial-grade AI finding
missing computer chips on your screen in just a few minutes.

*** PREREQUISITE: You must have Python installed on your computer.
If you don't have it, go to Python.org, click Download, and install it.
(IMPORTANT for Windows: During installation, make sure to check the box
that says "Add Python to PATH" at the very bottom before clicking Install).

PHASE 1: DOWNLOAD THE PROJECT TO YOUR COMPUTER

Go to the GitHub repository link provided to you.

Look for the big green button that says "<> Code". Click it.

Click "Download ZIP".

Once downloaded, find the ZIP file, right-click it, and select "Extract All".

Move this extracted folder (named NeuroSpector-main) to your Desktop so it is easy to find.

PHASE 2: OPEN THE "HACKER" TERMINAL

We need to give your computer some text commands. Here is the easiest way
to open the terminal exactly where we need it:

FOR WINDOWS USERS:

Open the NeuroSpector-main folder on your Desktop.

Click on the long white address bar at the very top of the window (where it says something like This PC > Desktop > NeuroSpector-main).

Delete all the text in that bar, type the letters cmd, and press Enter.

A black terminal window will pop up.

FOR MAC/LINUX USERS:

Find the NeuroSpector-main folder on your Desktop.

Right-click the folder and select "New Terminal at Folder" (Mac) or "Open in Terminal" (Linux).

PHASE 3: SET UP THE AI ENVIRONMENT (One-Time Setup)

Don't be intimidated by the black screen! Just copy and paste these commands
one by one, pressing Enter after each one. Wait for a command to finish
before pasting the next one.

FOR WINDOWS:

python -m venv yolo_env

yolo_env\Scripts\activate

pip install -r requirements.txt

FOR MAC/LINUX:

python3 -m venv yolo_env

source yolo_env/bin/activate

pip install -r requirements.txt

(Note: Step 3 might take a few minutes as it downloads the AI brain files).

PHASE 4: RUN THE AI MODEL!

Now for the fun part. We are going to tell the AI to look at the sample
pictures inside the test/images/ folder and find all the missing components.

Copy and paste this exact command and press Enter:

yolo task=detect mode=predict model=runs/detect/train/weights/best.pt source=test/images/ conf=0.40 save=True

What you just did:

model=...best.pt : You told it to use the trained "Brain" file.

source=test/images/ : You gave it the pictures to look at.

save=True : You told it to draw boxes and save the results for you to look at.

PHASE 5: SEE THE RESULTS

When the terminal stops printing text, it's done!

Go back to your normal Windows/Mac file explorer.

Open the NeuroSpector-main folder.

Navigate to: runs -> detect -> predict

Open the images inside! You will see the AI has drawn colored bounding boxes around every missing pin, chip, and capacitor, along with a percentage of how confident it is.

BONUS: TEST IT ON YOUR OWN WEBCAM

Want to see it work in real-time? Hold an Arduino board (or a picture of
one on your phone) up to your laptop's webcam, and run this command:

yolo task=detect mode=predict model=runs/detect/train/weights/best.pt source=0 show=True

A video window will pop up showing you what the AI sees right now!
(Press 'q' on your keyboard to close the window when you are done).
